Driving in the Netherlands

This page gives some tips about driving laws in the Netherlands for foreign drivers.

Right of way

There are two types of intersections in the Netherlands: unequal and equal intersections.

Unequal intersections are intersections between a main road and a side road of lesser importance. Vehicles coming from the side road have to give way to vehicles on the main road. When you see a sign and 'shark teeth' painted on the road, such as in the picture, you need to give way to all traffic from the left and right (including bicycles).

A sign indicates you have priority over traffic coming from the left and right. Traffic on the side streets will have shark teeth and a sign .

In addition, sign indicates that you are on a priority road with right of way over all side streets. Again, traffic on the side streets will have shark teeth and a sign

By the way, traffic signs on crossings with traffic lights can be ignored if the traffic lights are working: these signs only apply when the lights are out of order. In the picture, the traffic lights are working, so the sign does not apply.

On equal intersections, you need to give way to all vehicles (including bicycles) coming from the right. These intersections are normally only found on smaller roads, such as residential districts.

Sometimes side streets enter the main road by driving over a raised pavement. If you have to cross the raised pavement (both when entering and leaving the side street), you need to give way to all traffic, including pedestrians.

When turning right, you need to give way to pedestrians on the sidewalk, and bicyclists on the road or bicycle path. Always check your mirrors and do a shoulder check. In the image, the car should give way to the cyclist on bicycle path.

When turning left, you need to give way to opposite traffic, as well as pedestrians and bicyclists. In the image, when turning left you should give way to the gray car, the cyclist on the bike path, and the pedestrians.

Roundabouts

On multilane roundabouts (turbo-roundabouts), it is important to choose the correct lane. After that, just keep following your lane and you will end up at the correct place.

With roundabouts, you need to give way at two places. First, when approaching the roundabout, look left to give way to any traffic already on the roundabout. The bicycle paths might or might not be included (pay attention to the shark teeth).

When leaving the roundabout, you often (but not always) need to give way to bicyclists and pedestrians. Pay attention to the shark teeth and zebra crossing.
